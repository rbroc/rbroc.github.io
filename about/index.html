About

Hi there! I am a postdoctoral researcher with Tal Yarkoni at Psychoinformatics Lab, Department of Psychology, UT Austin.

I hold a PhD in Cognitive Neuroscience from the Department of Linguistics, Cognitive Science and Semiotics and the Interacting Minds Centre at Aarhus University. I worked under the supervision of Mikkel Wallentin, Kristian Tylén and Kenny Coventry on the neural correlates of spatial demonstratives, using naturalistic imaging, large-scale behavioral experiments and interactive two-persons paradigms.

My PhD project was part of the Marie Skłodowska-Curie Training Network DCOMM, funded by the European Union under the Horizon2020 framework.

I have had the chance to study and work all around Europe. I started from a BA in Philosophy at the University of Palermo, where I got more and more interested in philosophy of language. Then, I did my MSc in Cognitive Science at the Mind and Brain Centre (CIMeC), University of Trento, with a focus on computational linguistics.

During my Masters, I worked as a Research Assistant at the Language and Cognition Department at the Max Planck Institute for Psycholinguistics in Nijmegen, the Netherlands and at the Department of Experimental Psychology at University College London.

I spend a great deal of my free time between literature, music, and politics. I occasionally write fiction and do some freelance culture journalism (I have written for Doppiozero, Indiscreto, Il lavoro culturale). You can occasionally also hear me playing bass, guitar or piano, alone or in a band, or bragging about how proud I am of being a EU citizen.
